GRE Vocab Guide

Group 1

  • Abound - Mnemonic: Imagine things are in such large numbers they are about to bound out of the container. Definition: large numbers or amounts.

  • Amorphous - Mnemonic: "a" (without) + "morph" (shape). Literally, without shape. Definition: without a clearly defined shape or form.

  • Austere - Mnemonic: Sounds like "Oh, steer clear." You would steer clear of a person who is very strict and lives without luxuries. Definition: strict in manner, having no comforts or luxuries.

  • Belie - Mnemonic: To be a lie. Something that belies the facts is a lie and contradicts the truth. Definition: fail to give true impression of something, contradict, fail to justify.

  • Capricious - Mnemonic: Think of capri pants. Wearing them might be a sudden, unpredictable fashion choice depending on your mood. Definition: sudden change of mood, unpredictable, fickle.

  • Cerebral - Mnemonic: Relates to the cerebrum part of the brain. So, it's about being intellectual. Definition: of the cerebrum of the brain, intellectual rather than physical.

  • Congenial - Mnemonic: "con" (with) + "genial" (friendly). A person who is friendly and pleasant to be with. Definition: pleasant or agreeable because suited to one's taste.

  • Conspicuous - Mnemonic: "con" (with) + "spic" (see). Something you can easily see with your eyes is conspicuous. Definition: clearly visible, attracting notice or attention.

  • Cursory - Mnemonic: Think of a computer cursor, which moves hastily across the page. A cursory glance is a hasty one. Definition: hasty and therefore not through or detailed.

  • Daunting - Mnemonic: Sounds like haunting. A difficult task can be haunting and discouraging. Definition: difficult to deal with, discourage through fear.

  • Deify - Mnemonic: From deity, which means god. To deify someone is to treat them like a god. Definition: worship or regard as a god.

  • Didactic - Mnemonic: Sounds like "didactic-tic-tic," like a professor ticking off points while teaching, sometimes in a patronizing way. Definition: instructive, particularly so as to appear patronizing.

  • Disseminate - Mnemonic: "dis" (apart) + "seminate" (seed). Imagine spreading or scattering seeds widely. Definition: spread widely.

  • Feasible - Mnemonic: Sounds like "easy-ble." Something that is able to be done easy. Definition: possible to do easily or conveniently.

  • Flout - Mnemonic: Sounds like flung out. You have flung out the rules, showing you openly disregard them. Definition: openly disregard, mock, scoff.

  • Homogeneous - Mnemonic: "homo" means same. A group of things of the same kind. Definition: of the same kind.

  • Humdrum - Mnemonic: Sounds like someone humming a dull drum beat over and over. It's boring and monotonous. Definition: lacking excitement or variety; boringly monotonous.

  • Insipid - Mnemonic: "in" (not) + "sipid" (tasty). If you wouldn't even sip it, it must be tasteless and uninteresting. Definition: tasteless, lacking interest.

  • Loquacious - Mnemonic: The root "loq" means talk (like in eloquent). Loquacious means very talkative. Definition: talkative.

  • Misanthropic - Mnemonic: "mis" (hate) + "anthro" (mankind). Someone who dislikes people. Definition: having or showing dislike of other people.

  • Misnomer - Mnemonic: "mis" (wrong) + "nomer" (name). A wrong or inaccurate name. Definition: a wrong or inaccurate name or designation.

  • Negligent - Mnemonic: Similar to neglect. Someone who neglects their duties is failing to take proper care. Definition: failing to take proper care over something.

  • Obsequious - Mnemonic: Sounds like obsessed with pleasing. An obedient, servile follower. Definition: obedient to an excessive or servile degree.

  • Placate - Mnemonic: You can often placate someone with a plate of cake. Definition: make less angry.

  • Proclivity - Mnemonic: "pro" (forward) + "clivity" (slope). You have a forward slope or natural tendency towards something. Definition: a tendency to choose or do something regularly.

  • Puerile - Mnemonic: Sounds like purely silly. It means childishly silly. Definition: childishly silly.

  • Quixotic - Mnemonic: Comes from Don Quixote, the famously idealistic and impractical knight. Definition: extremely idealistic, unrealistic, impractical.

  • Spendthrift - Mnemonic: Someone who spends with no sense of thrift (careful use of money). Definition: a person who spends money in an irresponsible way.

  • Taciturn - Mnemonic: Sounds like they reluctantly take their turn to speak, but say little. Definition: saying little.

  • Wary - Mnemonic: Sounds like beware. You should be cautious or wary of things you are told to beware of. Definition: feeling or showing caution about possible dangers.

Group 2

  • Adulterate - Mnemonic: Adding something to a product as an adult might do to "fix" it, but it actually makes the quality poorer. Definition: render poorer in quality by adding another substance.

  • Advocate - Mnemonic: An advocate (like a lawyer) is someone who publicly supports a cause. Definition: publicly recommend or support.

  • Aggrandize - Mnemonic: To make something seem more grand or bigger than it is. Definition: increase power, status, reputation of someone.

  • Alacrity - Mnemonic: Sounds like a lack of pity. If someone is working with brisk readiness, they have no time for pity. Definition: brisk and cheerful readiness.

  • Ambivalent - Mnemonic: "ambi" means both (like ambidextrous). Having both feelings about something at once. Definition: having mixed feelings or contradictory ideas.

  • Ameliorate - Mnemonic: Sounds like Emilio rated the situation as being better after it improved. Definition: make something bad better.

  • Amenable - Mnemonic: Sounds like amend-able. Something that can be easily changed or is open to suggestion. Definition: open to suggestions, easily persuaded, susceptible.

  • Anachronistic - Mnemonic: "ana" (against) + "chronos" (time). Something that is against the correct time period. Definition: belonging to a period other than that being portrayed conspicuously old-fashioned.

  • Audacious - Mnemonic: Having the audacity to take bold, surprising risks. Definition: willingness to take surprisingly bold risks, showing impudent lack of respect.

  • Avaricious - Mnemonic: Sounds like have our riches. Someone who is avaricious is extremely greedy and wants to have all the riches. Definition: showing extreme greed for wealth or material gain.

  • Banal - Mnemonic: Sounds like you should ban all things that are so obvious and boring. Definition: lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring.

  • Benign - Mnemonic: Think "be nice." Benign means gentle and kind. Definition: gentle and kind, not harmful to the environment.

  • Brazen - Mnemonic: Sounds like blazing. To act with a blazing, shameless boldness. Definition: bold and without shame.

  • Calumny - Mnemonic: Sounds like column. Imagine a gossip columnist writing false, slanderous statements about people. Definition: false and slanderous statement about someone.

  • Candid - Mnemonic: A candid photo is one that is truthful and not posed. Definition: truthful and straightforward.

  • Castigate - Mnemonic: Sounds like caste gate. In some societies, people of a lower caste were severely reprimanded if they passed through the wrong gate. Definition: reprimand severely.

  • Caustic - Mnemonic: Sounds like it cost him his friend, because of his sarcastic and bitter words. Definition: sarcastic in a bitter way, chemical.

  • Construed - Mnemonic: You construct meaning from what someone says. How you interpret it is how you construe it. Definition: interpret in a particular way.

  • Contrite - Mnemonic: "Count right." The sinner wants to count his sins right so he can express remorse for them. Definition: feeling or expressing remorse that one has done wrong.

  • Convoluted - Mnemonic: Like a "convoy" of trucks that has gotten twisted and is now difficult to follow. Definition: extremely complex and difficult to follow and twisted.

  • Covet - Mnemonic: To cover it and desire to possess it, even though it belongs to someone else. Definition: yearn to possess something belonging to another.

  • Craven - Mnemonic: Sounds like a scared raven, lacking in courage. Definition: lacking in courage, cowardly.

  • Decorum - Mnemonic: "Decorate them" properly. Behavior that is in keeping with good taste and propriety. Definition: behavior in keeping with good taste and propriety.

  • Deft - Mnemonic: The opposite of daft. Someone who is deft is skillful and clever. Definition: demonstrating skill and cleverness.

  • Demur - Mnemonic: Sounds like demure, or shy. A demure person might raise objections or show reluctance. Definition: raise objections or show reluctance.

  • Derivative - Mnemonic: In calculus, a derivative is derived from another function. In art, a derivative work is imitative of another. Definition: imitative of the work of another.

  • Desiccate - Mnemonic: Think of a desert cake that has been left in the sun to become completely dry. Definition: cause to become completely dry.

  • Diatribe - Mnemonic: "dia" (through) + "tribe". He verbally attacked the whole tribe. Definition: a forceful and bitter verbal attack.

  • Incredulous - Mnemonic: In-credible. You find it so incredible that you are unable to believe it. Definition: unwilling or unable to believe something.

  • Ingenuous - Mnemonic: A genuine person is often innocent and unsuspecting. Definition: innocent, naive or unsuspecting.

Group 3

  • Abate - Mnemonic: Sounds like rebate. A rebate reduces the price, just as abate means to become less intense. Definition: become less intense or widespread, remove or reduce law.

  • Abjure - Mnemonic: To injure your old beliefs by renouncing them. Definition: solemnly renounce (a belief, cause or claim).

  • Anomalous - Mnemonic: "a" (not) + "nomalous" (normal). Not normal, deviating from the standard. Definition: deviating from what is standard.

  • Antipathy - Mnemonic: Anti-sympathy. If you have no sympathy for someone, you probably have a deep-seated feeling of aversion to them. Definition: a deep-seated feeling of aversion.

  • Arcane - Mnemonic: Sounds like our cane. The secret knowledge of how to use our family's magic cane is understood by few. Definition: understood by few, mysterious or secret.

  • Arduous - Mnemonic: Sounds like "hard for us." Something that is hard for us to do is arduous. Definition: involving or requiring strenuous effort, tiring.

  • Artless - Mnemonic: Less art, more natural. Without deception or pretentiousness. Definition: without deception, effort or pretentiousness, natural.

  • Ascetic - Mnemonic: Sounds like "acidic." An ascetic person lives a life of severe self-discipline, which might feel like putting acid on worldly pleasures. Definition: severe self-discipline, austere.

  • Assuage - Mnemonic: Sounds like a massage, which can make an unpleasant feeling of stress less intense. Definition: satisfy, make an unpleasant feeling less intense.

  • Betray - Mnemonic: To be a traitor. Not just disloyal, but also unintentionally revealing something. Definition: gravely disloyal, unintentionally reveal, be evidence of.

  • Bucolic - Mnemonic: Sounds like blue colic. You get a stomach ache from eating too many blueberries in the pleasant countryside. Definition: relating to the pleasant aspects of the countryside.

  • Burgeon - Mnemonic: Like a burg (town) that begins to grow rapidly. Definition: begin to grow or increase rapidly, flourish.

  • Cacophonous - Mnemonic: Sounds like a cacophony of horrible sounds, like a coffin falling down the stairs. Definition: involving or producing harsh sounds.

  • Canonize - Mnemonic: To put someone or something into the official canon of important works. Definition: treat of great significance, place in or regard as belonging to a canon of literacy or artistic works.

  • Censure - Mnemonic: Related to censor. If you disapprove of something, you might censor it. Censure is the act of expressing severe disapproval. Definition: express severe disapproval, especially in formal statement.

  • Chicanery - Mnemonic: Sounds like chickens running around in a tricky, deceptive way to avoid being caught. Definition: use of deception or subterfuge.

  • Coalesce - Mnemonic: "co" (together). Like coal pieces coming together to form one mass. Definition: come together to from one mass or whole.

  • Cogent - Mnemonic: A co-agent in an argument must be clear, logical, and convincing. Definition: clear, logical and convincing.

  • Compelling - Mnemonic: It is so interesting that it is compelling you to pay attention. Definition: evoking interest in a powerful irresistible way.

  • Contend - Mnemonic: A contender in a fight struggles to surmount difficulty. Definition: struggle to surmount a difficulty, assert something as a position in an argument.

  • Copious - Mnemonic: There are enough copies for everyone because the supply is abundant. Definition: abundant in supply.

  • Cosmopolitan - Mnemonic: A cosmos is the whole universe. A cosmopolitan place has a mixture of cultures from all over. Definition: mixture of cultures.

  • Deference - Mnemonic: Showing polite respect by deferring to someone else's opinion or wishes. Definition: polite submission and respect.

  • Desultory - Mnemonic: "De-result." Any action that lacks a plan or purpose will not lead to a result. Definition: lacking plan, purpose or enthusiasm, occurring randomly.

  • Diffident - Mnemonic: Difficult to be confident. A diffident person is shy due to a lack of self-confidence. Definition: modest or shy because of a lack of self-confidence.

  • Dilatory - Mnemonic: Sounds like delay-tory. Intended to cause a delay. Definition: slow to act, intended to cause delay.

  • Equivocate - Mnemonic: To speak in a way that sounds equally vocal for two opposing sides, using ambiguous language to avoid commitment. Definition: use ambiguous language.

  • Polarize - Mnemonic: To divide into two opposite poles, like the North and South Pole. Definition: divide or cause it divide into two sharply contrasting group.

  • Prodigal - Mnemonic: A proud gal who spends money recklessly to show off. Definition: spending money recklessly.

  • Verbose - Mnemonic: A person full of verbs, nouns, and adjectives; using more words than needed. Definition: using or expressed in more words that are needed.

Group 4

  • Abstain - Mnemonic: "ab" (away) + "stain". Stay away from getting a stain on your record by restraining yourself. Definition: restrain oneself from doing.

  • Approbation - Mnemonic: Sounds like approve. It means approval or praise. Definition: approval or praise.

  • Cherish - Mnemonic: Sounds like cherry-ish. You would protect and care for a sweet cherry lovingly. Definition: protect and care for lovingly.

  • Corroborate - Mnemonic: Sounds like co-robber-ate. Your co-robber ate the same food as you, which helps confirm your alibi. Definition: confirm or give support to a statement, theory or finding.

  • Disparate - Mnemonic: "This parrot" and "that parrot" are disparate; they are essentially different. Definition: essentially different in kind, notable to be compared.

  • Emulate - Mnemonic: Sounds like imitate. To imitate someone in order to match or surpass them. Definition: match or surpass a person or achievement, typically by imitation.

  • Enervate - Mnemonic: "e" (out of) + "nerv" (nerve/energy). To take the energy out of someone. Definition: make someone feel drained of energy, lacking in energy.

  • Ephemeral - Mnemonic: Sounds like "a funeral." It's a reminder that life is short and lasts for a very short time. Definition: lasting for a very short time.

  • Fervid - Mnemonic: Sounds like fever. When you have a fever, you are intensely hot. Fervid means intensely enthusiastic. Definition: intensely enthusiastic or passionate, especially to an excessive degree.

  • Garrulous - Mnemonic: Sounds like gorillas that are excessively talkative about trivial matters. Definition: excessively talkative, especially on trivial matters.

  • Incendiary - Mnemonic: An incense stick that you light can stir up smoke, just as an incendiary comment can stir up conflict. Definition: tending to stir up conflict.

  • Inimical - Mnemonic: Sounds like enemy-cal. An enemy is unfriendly and hostile. Definition: tending to obstruct, unfriendly hostile.

  • Intimate - Mnemonic: When you are intimate with someone, you are closely acquainted. Intimate knowledge of a subject is detailed and thorough. Definition: closely acquainted, familiar, detailed or thorough.

  • Invigorate - Mnemonic: To put vigor or energy into someone. Definition: give strength or energy to.

  • Mitigate - Mnemonic: Sounds like middle gate. The guard opened the middle gate to make the long line less severe. Definition: make something bad less severe, serious or painful.

  • Obsolete - Mnemonic: This technology is absolutely out of date. Definition: out of date.

  • Opaque - Mnemonic: Sounds like it's unclear; you can't see through it. Not transparent. Definition: unclear, not transparent.

  • Paradigmatic - Mnemonic: A paradigm is a typical example of something. Definition: serving as a typical example of something.

  • Pedantic - Mnemonic: Imagine a pedal antique expert who is excessively concerned with minor details about old bicycle pedals. Definition: excessively concerned with minor details, over-scrupulous.

  • Placid - Mnemonic: Sounds like placate. A placid person is calm and not easily upset. Definition: not easily upset or excited, calm and peaceful.

  • Polemical - Mnemonic: Sounds like political. Political writing is often strongly critical. Definition: of or involving strongly critical writing or speech.

  • Precipitate - Mnemonic: Sounds like pre-anticipate. To cause something to happen before you anticipated it, suddenly. Definition: cause to happen suddenly, unexpectedly or premature.

  • Profundity - Mnemonic: From profound, meaning great depth. Definition: great depth of insight or knowledge.

  • Prophetic - Mnemonic: A prophet accurately predicts the future. Definition: accurately predicting what will happen in future.

  • Prudent - Mnemonic: A true student is often prudent, acting with care for the future. Definition: acting with or showing care and thought for future.

  • Punctilious - Mnemonic: Sounds like punctual. A punctual person shows great attention to the detail of being on time. Definition: showing great attention to detail or correct behavior.

  • Recondite - Mnemonic: Sounds like you have to re-conduct the experiment because the subject is little known and abstruse. Definition: little known, abstruse.

  • Scrupulous - Mnemonic: A sculptor must be very careful and thorough with details. Definition: careful, thorough, and extremely attentive to details.

  • Tranquil - Mnemonic: Think of tranquilizers, which make you calm and free from disturbance. Definition: free from disturbance, calm.

  • Vacillate - Mnemonic: Sounds like vaccine late. You might waver between different opinions on whether to get a vaccine late. Definition: waver between different opinions or actions, be indecisive.

Group 5

  • Aloof - Mnemonic: Sounds like a roof. Someone who stays on a roof is distant from everyone else. Definition: not friendly, distant, detached.

  • Clangor - Mnemonic: The clang of a hammer makes a continuously loud banging sound. Definition: a continuously loud banging or ringing sound.

  • Conventional - Mnemonic: A convention is a traditional meeting; something conventional follows traditional forms. Definition: following traditional forms and genres.

  • Debunk - Mnemonic: To remove the junk, or falseness, from an idea. Definition: expose the falseness or hollowness of an idea or belief.

  • Diminutive - Mnemonic: Diminish means to make smaller. Diminutive means extremely small. Definition: extremely or unusually small.

  • Discernible - Mnemonic: If you can discern it, you are able to see it. Perceptible. Definition: able to be discerned, perceptible, visible, noticeable.

  • Enigmatic - Mnemonic: Sounds like the Enigma machine from WWII, which was difficult to understand. Mysterious. Definition: difficult to understand, mysterious.

  • Estranged - Mnemonic: To make someone a stranger. No longer close to someone. Definition: no longer close to someone, alienated.

  • Extravagant - Mnemonic: Extra-vagrant. Going extra beyond what is necessary; excessive. Definition: excessive, elaborate.

  • Fanciful - Mnemonic: Full of fancy. Over-imaginative and unrealistic. Definition: over-imaginative, unrealistic, highly ornamental.

  • Frivolous - Mnemonic: Sounds like free-for-us. Things that are free are sometimes treated without serious purpose. Definition: carefree, not having any serious purpose.

  • Heterogeneous - Mnemonic: "hetero" means different. Diverse in character or content. Definition: diverse in character or content.

  • Imperious - Mnemonic: Someone who acts like an emperor is arrogant and domineering. Definition: arrogant and domineering.

  • Impertinent - Mnemonic: The opposite of pertinent (relevant). Someone who makes irrelevant comments shows a lack of respect. Definition: not showing proper respect, irrelevant.

  • Invasive - Mnemonic: Weeds that invade your garden tend to spread very quickly and harmfully. Definition: tending to spread very quickly or undesirably, harmfully.

  • Irresolute - Mnemonic: Not resolute. Someone who is not resolute is uncertain and hesitant. Definition: showing or feeling hesitancy; uncertain.

  • Laudable - Mnemonic: To give an applaud-able performance, which is deserving of praise. Definition: deserving praise and commendation.

  • Lax - Mnemonic: Sounds like relax. If you are too relaxed about the rules, you are not being strict enough. Definition: not sufficiently strict or careful, negligible / of muscle(relaxed).

  • Marginalize - Mnemonic: To put someone in the margins, treating them as insignificant. Definition: treat a person, group as insignificant or peripheral.

  • Panache - Mnemonic: Sounds like a mustache worn with flamboyant confidence and style. Definition: flamboyant confidence of style or manner.

  • Plodding - Mnemonic: The sound "plod, plod, plod" suggests a slow-moving and unexciting walk. Definition: slow moving and unexciting.

  • Prosaic - Mnemonic: The opposite of poetic. Prose is ordinary writing, so prosaic means commonplace and unromantic. Definition: lacking originality, commonplace, unromantic.

  • Remedial - Mnemonic: A remedy is intended as a cure. Remedial classes are intended for students with learning difficulties. Definition: intended as cure/ provided or intended for children with learning difficulties.

  • Restive - Mnemonic: A person who cannot rest is unable to remain still, perhaps due to boredom. Definition: unable to remain still, silent, or submissive, especially because of boredom.

  • Sporadic - Mnemonic: Opposite of periodic. Happening infrequently and at scattered intervals. Definition: infrequent, scattered or isolated, occasional.

  • Stigmatize - Mnemonic: A stigma is a mark of disgrace. To stigmatize is to show great disapproval. Definition: great disapproval.

  • Undermine - Mnemonic: To dig a mine under the base of something to lessen its effectiveness. Definition: erode the base/ lessen the effectiveness.

  • Utterly - Mnemonic: You utter a complete thought. Utterly means completely. Definition: completely or without qualification, absolutely.

  • Weary - Mnemonic: You wear out your energy and become extremely tired. Definition: tired(extreme).

  • Zealous - Mnemonic: Sounds like jealous. A jealous person might show a passionate, or zealous, devotion to someone. Definition: showing zeal, fervent, passionate, fervid, ardent.

Group 6

  • Admonish - Mnemonic: A monk might admonish you, or warn you firmly, about your behavior. Definition: warn or reprimand someone firmly.

  • Aesthetic - Mnemonic: Relates to the appreciation of art and beauty. Definition: concerned with beauty.

  • Affectation - Mnemonic: To put on an affection that isn't real; a facade. Definition: pretension, facade.

  • Alleviate - Mnemonic: Sounds like a leave. Taking a leave from work can make suffering less severe. Definition: make suffering less severe.

  • Analogous - Mnemonic: Similar to an analogy, which shows how two things are comparable. Definition: comparable in certain respects.

  • Bolster - Mnemonic: A bolster pillow is used to support you. Definition: support or strength.

  • Chauvinistic - Mnemonic: From Nicolas Chauvin, a French soldier with excessive patriotism. Definition: displaying aggressive patriotism, displaying excessive support for their own cause, group or sex.

  • Connoisseur - Mnemonic: A connoisseur knows "can I sewer" this fabric? They are an expert judge. Definition: an expert judge in matters of taste.

  • Dissemble - Mnemonic: It's the opposite of resemble. You make your feelings not resemble what they truly are, thus concealing them. Definition: conceal or disguise one's true feelings or beliefs.

  • Dogged - Mnemonic: Like a dog that won't let go of a bone, showing grim persistence. Definition: showing tenacity and grim persistence.

  • Dupe - Mnemonic: If you are duped, you are a victim of a trick or deception. Definition: trick, a victim of deception.

  • Empirical - Mnemonic: Based on an empire of observed facts, not just theory. Definition: based on observation rather than theory.

  • Engender - Mnemonic: To generate a certain feeling or situation; to cause it. Definition: cause or give rise to a feeling situation.

  • Entitled - Mnemonic: You are given the title or legal right to something. Definition: give someone a legal right.

  • Pertinacious - Mnemonic: He pertains to his opinion. He holds firmly to his opinion. Definition: holding firmly to an opinion or a cause of action.

  • Presumptuous - Mnemonic: You presume it's okay, failing to observe the limits of what is appropriate. Definition: failing to observe limits of what is appropriate/brazen.

  • Probity - Mnemonic: A person with probity has strong moral principles, like someone who would never agree to a dishonest probe. Definition: having strong moral principles, honesty and decency.

  • Proliferate - Mnemonic: Like pro-life, which is associated with an increase in the number of people. Definition: increase rapidly in number, multiply.

  • Specious - Mnemonic: It seems plausible, but it's actually wrong. Definition: superficially plausible but actually wrong.

  • Spurious - Mnemonic: A spur-of-the-moment story is often false or fake. Definition: not being what it purports to be, false or fake.

  • Subjective - Mnemonic: Its about the subject (the person), not the object, so it's influenced by personal feelings. Definition: influenced by personal feelings, tastes, opinions.

  • Subvert - Mnemonic: "sub" (under) + "vert" (turn). To turn from under; to undermine power. Definition: undermine the power and authority of system.

  • Timorous - Mnemonic: A timid mouse is easily frightened. Definition: lack of confidence, easily frightened.

  • Tortuous - Mnemonic: Like a tortoise's path, full of twists and turns. Definition: full of twists and turns excessively complex.

  • Tractable - Mnemonic: It can be pulled by a tractor, meaning it's easy to control or influence. Definition: easy to control or influence, easy to deal with.

  • Transient - Mnemonic: Like a train, it's only here for a short time. Definition: lasting for a short time.

  • Ubiquitous - Mnemonic: Sounds like "you be quiet, I see us" everywhere. Present everywhere. Definition: present, appearing or found everywhere, omnipresent.

  • Underscore - Mnemonic: An underscore on a keyboard is used to emphasize a word. Definition: emphasize.

  • Venal - Mnemonic: You can pay this politician through your Venmo; they are open to bribery. Definition: bribery, corrupt.

  • Venerate - Mnemonic: Sounds like "when you rate" someone highly, you regard them with great respect. Definition: regard with great respect, reverence.

Group 7

  • Appease - Mnemonic: Offer a piece of cake to pacify someone. Definition: pacify or placate, assuage or satisfy.

  • Arbitrary - Mnemonic: It was a random choice, as if chosen by throwing a bit of tar at a map. Definition: based on random choice/ unrestrained and autocratic in the use of authority.

  • Archaic - Mnemonic: From archaeology, the study of very old things. Definition: very old or old-fashioned.

  • Clamorous - Mnemonic: Full of clamor or a loud, confused noise. Definition: making loud and confused noise/expressing or characterized by vehement protects or demands.

  • Dearth - Mnemonic: The opposite of earth's abundance; a scarcity or death of supply. Definition: a scarcity or lack of something.

  • Explicable - Mnemonic: Able to be explained. Definition: able to be accounted for or understood, explainable.

  • Hyperbole - Mnemonic: A hyper bowl that's exaggerated to be the size of a stadium. Definition: exaggerated statements or claims not meant to be taken literally.

  • Immutable - Mnemonic: Not mutable; unable to be changed or mutated. Definition: unable to be changed.

  • Indefatigable - Mnemonic: Not fatigable; unable to become tired; tireless. Definition: persisting tireless, tireless, untiring.

  • Indolent - Mnemonic: Sounds like indoor lent. Someone who stays indoors all of Lent is probably lazy. Definition: lazy, idle/ (of medicine) no or little pain.

  • Insular - Mnemonic: Related to insulation, which keeps things separate. Uninterested in outside cultures or ideas. Definition: uninterested in cultures, ideas, or peoples outside one's own experience, lacking contact with other people.

  • Intransigent - Mnemonic: An in-transit agent who is unwilling to change your travel plans. Uncompromising. Definition: unwilling to change one's views, uncompromising.

  • Intrepid - Mnemonic: Not trepid (fearful). Fearless and adventurous. Definition: fearless, adventurous (often used for humorous effect).

  • Irreverent - Mnemonic: Not reverent. Showing a lack of respect for serious things. Definition: showing lack of respect for people or things that are generally taken seriously.

  • Loathe - Mnemonic: You feel so much disgust it makes you want to lather yourself clean. Definition: intense dislike or disgust for.

  • Malign - Mnemonic: To speak about someone in a way that is mal (bad or evil). Definition: evil in nature, speak about someone in a spitefully critical manner.

  • Malleable - Mnemonic: Like malleable metal, which can be easily shaped. Easily influenced. Definition: easily influenced, pliable.

  • Neophyte - Mnemonic: A "new fighter" in a subject or religion. A beginner. Definition: a person who is new to a subject, activity, or religion.

  • Plastic - Mnemonic: Plastic can be easily shaped. Definition: easily shaped/artificial/offering scope for creativity.

  • Platitude - Mnemonic: A phrase that falls flat because it has been used too often. Definition: used too often to be interesting or thoughtful.

  • Prescient - Mnemonic: "pre" (before) + "science" (knowledge). Having knowledge of events before they happen. Definition: showing knowledge of events before they take place.

  • Pristine - Mnemonic: Looks like it was just cleaned by Mr. Clean; spotless. Definition: spotless, clean and fresh, immaculate.

  • Reproach - Mnemonic: When you re-approach someone to express your disapproval. Definition: expression of disapproval.

  • Robust - Mnemonic: Like a robot, strong and able to withstand adverse conditions. Definition: vigorous/ able to withstand adverse conditions/forceful.

  • Salubrious - Mnemonic: You salute a place that is healthy and pleasant. Definition: healthy, pleasant.

  • Presage - Mnemonic: A "sage" gives a "pre-message" or presage about the future.

    Definition : A sign or warning that something bad will happen.

  • Prescribe - Mnemonic: "Pre" (before) + "scribe" (write). A doctor writes it down before you can get the medicine.

    Definition : 1. To authorize the use of medicine. 2. To recommend an action as beneficial.

  • Prevail - Mnemonic: To prevail is to "prove" you're more powerful and win.

    Definition : To prove more powerful than opposing forces; be victorious.

  • Propitiate - Mnemonic: To propitiate a "prophet," you might give them a pleasing gift.

    Definition : To win or regain the favor of a god, spirit, or person by doing something that pleases them.

  • Recapitulate - Mnemonic: To "re-cap" the main points.

    Definition : To summarize and state again the main points of.
